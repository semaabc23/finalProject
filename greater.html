detrimental over time

Perception is everything. In the world of social media especially, perception of self and others is what governs what is posted, shared, and circulated on social media. Likes and comments dictate who is superior and who’s life seems to be more entertaining. In the root of it, people are driven by competition – competition for who can appear to be more desirable and has created the best ideal version of themselves on social media. In the end, social media warps our perception of our own lives and also the perception of those around us. It makes people feel a sense of inadequacy as they view how “ideal” the life they see on the screens in their hands. They are overcome by a feeling of not doing enough or being interesting enough.

As we lay out the lens for others to view us through, we convince ourselves we are the person we paint ourselves to be. When looking back at our memories, we often wish we were back in the “good old days,” not realizing that we were probably experiencing some hardship in our past. People fail to remember their past perfectly as they tend to only capture the happy moments and post those only. This further interferes with our own perception of our memories. We are convinced because we seem to be smiling and enjoying life in the pictures on our Instagram feeds or our Facebook memories. Elissa Gabbert discusses this idea of perception of ourselves in her essay, “Picture Yourself Happy”. She says taking photographs of herself allows for her to “confirm to myself that I am who I think I am." However, as this image gets tampered with in the process of perfecting it to share with others, it loses the original value and becomes almost like a second, different, version of what actually happened. The image of ourselves we once wanted others to believe ends up convincing us and altering the truth of our past in a sense.

People are convinced that they are only as interesting as what they put forth about themselves to the public. They are focused on trying to make themselves as interesting as they can by living life through a screen and failing to appreciate the moment when it happens and end up appreciating making the memory rather than having it. In the midst of it all, people are losing their sense of reality and allowing themselves to get lost in the darkness of social media.

TIP #1

Try not to be wrapped up in the pressure to adhere to the unrealistic standards that social media has put forth.

TIP #2

Try to keep in mind that what you see on social media does not accurately portray one's life and they have hardships they conceal just as you may do.

TIP #3

Remind yourself that you don't need to put forth content that will be the most liked or shared. Rather, focus on connecting and interacting with others.

TIP #4

Take a break sometimes from your virtual life and focus on enjoying and living your real life.
