age of social media

With every golden age, comes a dark age following it. Now that we have peaked in the social media age, are we approaching the dark age of it? Nearly 2.7 billion people around the globe are social media users. These accounts span a wide range of platforms ranging from Instagram to Twitter to Facebook. The recent years have been the golden years of social media with viral videos circulating the internet and social movements. Recently, however, there’s been a surge of negativity across social media as people are becoming more and more reliant on it and living through social media rather living with social media.

how social media dictates our day to day life

Social media has grown past just a means of communication, it has come to dictate nearly every aspect of our lives. People rely on it to not only socialize, but to seek a romantic partner, date, form sexual relationships, and even find careers.

Especially recently with a global pandemic driving many to resort to communicating and socializing remotely, people have become more reliant on social media than ever before. Every day we interact with carefully curated profiles which don’t completely portray the person behind the account.

the problem

The race to make oneself seem more desirable on social media has led to a false sense of authenticity. People feel pressured to curate “ideal” identities for their social media and dating profiles as they are a part of a culture that is driven by competition to get the most likes, comments, and shares. In the end it is as if that are competeing to have the “perfect” feed/timeline that would reflect an "ideal" life that they want others to believe they are living while trying to mask what is real.
